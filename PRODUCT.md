# Product

## Register

product

## Users

OakAttest is for ASD-registered IRAP assessor firms and the client organisations they assess. Primary users are lead assessors, assessors, assessor administrators, client administrators, client contributors, and read-only observers such as peer reviewers or ASD liaisons. They work with sensitive assessment material, evidence, findings, certification artefacts, and tenant-level audit obligations.

## Product Purpose

OakAttest runs the IRAP lifecycle from scoping through evidence, fieldwork, findings, certification, and ongoing compliance. Success means an assessor firm can create an organisation workspace, invite colleagues and clients, run engagements against an ISM revision, preserve evidence chain of custody, and produce defensible certification records from a self-installed or managed deployment.

## Brand Personality

The brand should feel assured, sober, and operational. It should project security without theatrical cyber styling, and it should feel like a professional assessment instrument rather than a marketing site. Copy is direct, Australian English, and plain about security, data residency, and responsibilities.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like a neon security operations centre, a consumer signup funnel, or a generic SaaS landing page. Avoid decorative gradients, glassmorphism, oversized hero sections inside the app, purple-blue tech palettes, and vague compliance theatre. Avoid flows that assume public SaaS email delivery is always configured, because self-installed deployments are a first-class use case.

## Design Principles

1. Organisation first: a user joins or creates an assessor firm workspace before the app asks them to do engagement work.
2. Trust through clarity: surface residency, auditability, roles, and chain-of-custody consequences in direct language.
3. Operational density: support repeated assessment work with predictable navigation, compact tables, and forms that scan quickly.
4. Security as posture, not friction: recommend MFA and strong controls without blocking self-installed setup unless the deployment chooses to enforce them.
5. Evidence over decoration: visual hierarchy should help users understand status, responsibility, and next action.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for product surfaces. Keep text readable in light-mode deployments, provide keyboard-visible focus states, avoid relying on colour alone for severity or status, and respect reduced-motion preferences. Forms must keep typed text readable against their backgrounds.
